## What This Guide Is For

This is a shared vocabulary for talking about growth at Sahaj. It describes **how impact evolves over time**: from learning and contributing safely, to shaping direction, building trust, and growing others. We intentionally do not use formal titles or grades — this guide exists to support honest conversations about expectations and feedback.

It's designed for **people in delivery roles**: engineers, tech leads, and those building products for clients. If you're in a different role, some principles may apply but the specific expectations won't map directly.

## What We Value

<!-- cards -->

### Technical Excellence as a Baseline, Not a Differentiator

Clean code, strong design, testing, and sound engineering practices are expected at every stage. Technical depth is necessary, but by itself it is not enough for long-term growth.

### Ownership Over Output

We value people who take responsibility for outcomes (technical, delivery, client, and organisational), not just for completing tasks.

### Consulting Mindset

Sahajeevis are expected to think like consultants: to understand problems deeply, remove ambiguity, propose solutions, and help stakeholders make better decisions. Doing what is asked without questioning unclear or risky assumptions has a natural growth ceiling.

### Delivery Excellence, Not Heroics

Sustainable, predictable delivery matters more than speed or firefighting. We optimize for long-term health of teams and systems.

### People and Culture as First-Class Responsibilities

Growing others, building trust, and strengthening teams are not "extra" activities. They are core to how impact scales at Sahaj.

### Transparency and Trust

In a culture with open conversations and open salaries, expectations must be explicit, observable, and fair.
