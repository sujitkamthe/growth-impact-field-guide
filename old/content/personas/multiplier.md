## Mindset

"My success is measured by how effective my team becomes without my constant presence."

**The question you're trusted to answer:** "How do we make this team effective?"

## Nature of Impact

- Single team (end-to-end)
- Capability multiplication
- Delivery + people + client outcomes

## Explicit Expectation

- Leads one team
- Owns team health, delivery, consulting quality, and technical bar

---

# Technical Delivery

## Expectations

- Defines and owns technical direction for the team
- Establishes shared patterns, libraries, and standards within the team
- Introduces tools or accelerators that improve the team's productivity
- Prevents recurring technical debt within the team's codebase
- Drives refactoring or modernization when it benefits the team
- Balances innovation with stability in the team's systems
- Anticipates future technical needs and risks for the engagement
- Aligns the team's technical strategy with business direction
- Mentors team members in advanced engineering concepts
- Raises the engineering quality bar within the team
- Acts as the team's technical escalation point

## Self-Assessment

- Has my work eliminated recurring technical problems within my team?
- Has the team's engineering quality improved because of standards I set?
- Can the team make good technical decisions when I'm not in the room?
- Do my technical choices create lasting improvements, not just short-term fixes?
- Am I raising the bar for the whole team, not just doing the hardest work myself?

---

# Consulting

## Expectations

- Leads solutioning for complex, high-ambiguity engagements
- Handles unclear or shifting goals with confidence
- Aligns multiple stakeholders toward a shared direction
- Anticipates stakeholder needs before they are stated
- Coaches others in consulting and problem-framing skills
- Navigates politics and power dynamics effectively
- Builds long-term stakeholder trust
- Shapes engagement direction beyond immediate scope
- Represents Sahaj as a trusted advisor

## Self-Assessment

- Do I influence direction beyond my formal role?
- Are others learning consulting skills by working with me?
- Can I operate effectively in politically complex environments?
- Do stakeholders trust my team to engage independently, or do they still route through me?
- Have I built a team that clients see as trusted advisors, not just executors?

---

# Delivery Excellence

## Expectations

- Improves delivery health within the team
- Introduces delivery practices that stick without enforcement
- Coaches the team on delivery planning and execution
- Balances multiple streams of work within the team
- Prevents burnout and chaos
- Improves the team's predictability

## Self-Assessment

- Has delivery become easier or harder because of me?
- Does my team feel safer and more predictable?
- Have I introduced practices that the team continues using without my enforcement?
- Can the team sustain its delivery pace during my absence?
- Am I preventing burnout, or just managing it after it happens?

---

# Mentorship & Talent Growth

## Expectations

- Develops talent within the team
- Builds a strong, self-sufficient team
- Creates learning structures and forums within the team
- Coaches team members to mentor each other
- Raises the overall capability bar of the team

## Self-Assessment

- Is the team stronger even when I step away?
- Am I creating future leaders?
- Can I name people who've grown into new responsibilities because of my coaching?
- Do I invest in the people who need it most, not just the ones who ask?
- Have I built mentoring into how the team operates, rather than doing it all myself?

---

# Communication & Influence

## Expectations

- Communicates the team's direction and progress clearly to stakeholders
- Shapes narratives and alignment within the team and with the client
- Builds credibility as the team's voice to stakeholders
- Represents the team effectively in cross-team or client discussions

## Self-Assessment

- Am I seen as a voice of clarity and trust within the team and with stakeholders?
- Can I align the team around a direction without forcing consensus?
- Do stakeholders trust the team's communication, or does everything route through me?
- Am I building the team's reputation through how we operate?
- Can I communicate difficult trade-offs to clients without escalating?
