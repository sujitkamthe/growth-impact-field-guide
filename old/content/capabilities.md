At Sahaj, expectations are described across five **capability areas**. These are best understood as **dimensions of impact**, not roles, grades, ratings, or levels. Capability areas are intentionally independent. Growth happens by **expanding impact across multiple areas over time**, not by maximising a single one.

This is not a scorecard. The radar shows where your impact currently shows up, not how good you are.

## How the Capability Areas Work Together

<!-- balance -->

No single capability area is sufficient on its own.

- Strong **Technical Delivery** without Consulting leads to execution-only roles
- Strong **Consulting** without Technical depth erodes trust
- Strong **Delivery** without Mentorship creates bottlenecks
- Strong **Influence** without substance creates noise

Sustainable growth at Sahaj comes from **balanced expansion across capability areas over time**, with different capability areas becoming more prominent at different stages.

**The goal is well-rounded growth, not perfection across every area.** Someone excelling in Technical Delivery, Consulting, and Delivery Excellence but needing improvement in Mentorship is doing well. They have a known growth area, not a crisis. Gaps become a concern when they're persistent across multiple areas, or when they're in capabilities critical to the scope you're operating at.

A gap is something to name and work on, not a verdict that overrides your strengths. That said, gaps become harder to carry as scope expands. Early on, they're less visible: the scope is smaller, the stakes are lower. Around Catalyst level, gaps become obvious. You're shaping direction, working with stakeholders, leading others. A blind spot in Consulting or Communication starts limiting how far your other strengths can take you.

The right response to a gap is awareness and intentional effort, not panic or self-demotion. Name it, discuss it with your team, and work on it.

### Can I Specialize and Still Grow?

Deep expertise is valued. You can grow significantly by going deep in Technical Delivery or Consulting while other areas stay adequate.

But over time, persistent gaps create tension. As your time in the organisation grows, expectations naturally grow with it. If your impact stays narrow while expectations broaden, a mismatch emerges: you're expected to operate at a level your weak areas can't support.

This is a natural consequence of how impact scales. Broader capabilities let you sustain growth longer. Persistent gaps don't block you immediately; they create friction later.

If you genuinely don't enjoy a capability area, that's worth naming. It's better to acknowledge it than to pretend. But understand the trade-off: staying narrow limits how far sustained growth can go.
